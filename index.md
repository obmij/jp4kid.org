#Welcome to JP4Kid.org

Now clearly, the importance of preventing violence 
is hard to stress. 
Enormously important. 
First of all, because of the harm and the pain it unnecessarily creates. 
Secondly, because its impact is not just immediate, but often enduring. 
We know from reliable scientific data that violence early 
on in a child's life can create what is now referred to as toxic stress. 
A level of stress that is very hard to overcome, 
that alerts the nervous system of the child 
later on in life to a toxic stress that impedes 
adaptive and positive development. 
We know that violence can generate long lasting trauma. 
And also we know that violence submitted to 
as a child can have intergenerational consequences. 
Children who have been treated violently and who have been abused often 
become abusive parents. 
So the importance of addressing this phenomenon and addressing it vigorously 
is hard to overstate.
